## AML/MDS — Haploidentical PBSC Transplantation, GVHD Prophylaxis

**Title:** A randomized trial of GVHD prophylaxis in haploidentical PBSC transplantation: ATG, PTCy, and low-dose combination therapy.  
**Journal:** Blood  
**Date:** 2026-Jun-25  
**PMID:** 41849227  
**DOI:** 10.1182/blood.2025032569  
**NCT:** NCT03608059

**Disease/Setting/Line:** AML and MDS with excess blasts (MDS-EB-1 or MDS-EB-2); haploidentical PBSC transplantation; adults aged 14–70 years.

**Phase | Design | N:** Phase III, open-label, randomized (2:1:1); N = 407 enrolled (ATG/PTCy n=185, ATG n=113, PTCy n=109).

**Intervention vs Comparator:**
- **ATG/PTCy:** low-dose ATG 5 mg/kg + posttransplant cyclophosphamide 50 mg/kg
- **Standard ATG:** 10 mg/kg total dose
- **PTCy:** 100 mg/kg total dose

**Coprimary Endpoints:**
1. Cumulative incidence (CI) of grade 2–4 acute GVHD by day 100:  
   - ATG/PTCy, ATG, and PTCy: **P = .210** (no significant difference; exact CI values not reported in abstract)
2. GVHD-free, relapse-free survival at 1 year: **not reported in abstract**

**Key Secondary Endpoints:**
- **Overall cGVHD incidence:** comparable across groups, P = .110 (exact rates not reported)  
- **2-year CI of moderate-to-severe cGVHD:**
  - ATG/PTCy: 17.4%
  - ATG: 17.3%
  - PTCy: 28.3%
  - **P = .095** (numerically lower in ATG/PTCy and ATG, but not statistically significant)
- **Survival outcomes:** no significant differences among groups (specific survival curves/rates not reported)  
- **Hematologic recovery:** CI of neutrophil and platelet recovery significantly higher in ATG/PTCy vs other groups, **P < .001**

**Safety:** Not reported in abstract.

**Median Follow-up:** not reported.

**Funding/Conflicts:** not reported in abstract.

**Potential Practice Relevance:** This phase III trial suggests three GVHD prophylaxis approaches yield similar efficacy for preventing grade 2–4 aGVHD and comparable survival in haploidentical PBSC transplantation for AML/MDS. The ATG/PTCy regimen showed faster hematologic recovery.  
*Caveats:* Open-label design; coprimary endpoint for 1-year GVHD-free, relapse-free survival not reported in abstract; cGVHD benefit did not reach statistical significance; specific survival and safety data absent from abstract.

## AG-120 (Ivosidenib) + Azacitidine vs. Placebo + Azacitidine in IDH1-Mutant AML

**NCT03173248** | ClinicalTrials.gov | Last updated 2026-06-29

**Disease / Setting / Line:**  
Newly diagnosed AML with IDH1 mutation; previously untreated; non-intensive therapy eligible

**Phase | Design | N:**  
Phase III, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled | N = 146 randomized (72 AG-120 + azacitidine; 74 placebo + azacitidine)

**Population / Key Eligibility:**  
Adults ≥18 years with newly diagnosed or untreated AML with IDH1 mutation; considered appropriate candidates for non-intensive therapy (azacitidine-based)

**Intervention:**  
AG-120 (ivosidenib) 500 mg orally once daily + azacitidine 75 mg/m² SC or IV, Days 1–7 or Days 1–5 and 8–9 of each 28-day cycle (minimum 6 cycles) vs. matched placebo + azacitidine (same schedule)

**Primary Endpoint + Result:**  
**Event-Free Survival (EFS)** defined as time from randomization until treatment failure, relapse, or death from any cause (treatment failure = failure to achieve CR by Week 24).  
- **AG-120 + AZA:** Median EFS = 0.03 months (95% CI: 0.03–11.01)  
- **Placebo + AZA:** Median EFS = 0.03 months (95% CI: not estimable due to low event count)  
- **Hazard Ratio = 0.33 (95% CI: 0.16–0.69); one-sided log-rank test p = 0.0011** (stratified by AML status and geographic region)  
*Interpretation:* AG-120 + azacitidine reduced risk of EFS event by 67% vs. placebo + azacitidine.

**Key Secondary Endpoints:**  
- **Overall Survival (OS):** not reported (reported as NOT_POSTED; anticipated posting date 2027-06)  
- **Complete Remission (CR) Rate:** not reported  
- **CR + CRh Rate:** not reported  
- **Objective Response Rate (ORR):** not reported  
All secondary efficacy endpoints anticipated for posting June 2027.

**Safety (Serious Adverse Events; frequency threshold ≥5%):**  
- **Deaths:** AG-120 + AZA 27/71 (38%); Placebo + AZA 45/73 (62%)  
- **Serious AEs, any grade:** AG-120 + AZA 49/71 (69%); Placebo + AZA 60/73 (82%)  
- **Grade ≥3 AE leaders:**  
  - Febrile neutropenia: AG-120 17/71 (24%), Placebo 20/73 (27%)  
  - Pneumonia (any grade SAE): AG-120 14/71 (20%), Placebo 16/73 (22%)  
  - Differentiation syndrome (any grade): AG-120 6/71 (8%), Placebo 1/73 (1%) [*special interest*]  
  - Pulmonary embolism (SAE): AG-120 3/71 (4%), Placebo 1/73 (1%)  
  - Intracranial hemorrhage (SAE): AG-120 2/71 (3%), Placebo 0/73  
- **Other notable Grade ≥3 AEs (any grade, frequency >10% in either arm):**  
  - Anemia: AG-120 22/71 (31%), Placebo 21/73 (29%)  
  - Neutropenia: AG-120 20/71 (28%), Placebo 12/73 (16%)  
  - Thrombocytopenia: AG-120 20/71 (28%), Placebo 14/73 (19%)  
  - Nausea: AG-120 30/71 (42%), Placebo 28/73 (38%)  
  - Vomiting: AG-120 28/71 (39%), Placebo 19/73 (26%)  
  - Constipation: AG-120 19/71 (27%), Placebo 38/73 (52%)  
  - Diarrhea: AG-120 25/71 (35%), Placebo 25/73 (34%)  
  - Pyrexia: AG-120 23/71 (32%), Placebo 29/73 (40%)  
  - Electrocardiogram QT prolonged: AG-120 14/71 (20%), Placebo 5/73 (7%) [*special interest*]  
  - Asthenia: AG-120 11/71 (15%), Placebo 24/73 (33%)  
  - Decreased appetite: AG-120 11/71 (15%), Placebo 19/73 (26%)  

**Median Follow-up:**  
Not explicitly reported in the results section.

**Funding / Conflicts:**  
Lead Sponsor: Institut de Recherches Internationales Servier (non-profit/academic sponsor class: OTHER). No individual investigator conflict-of-interest data provided in this record.

**Potential Practice Relevance:**  
EFS favored AG-120 + azacitidine with a statistically significant hazard ratio of 0.33 (p = 0.0011), suggesting a 67% reduction in risk of treatment failure, relapse, or death. However, **OS and other key secondary efficacy endpoints (CR rate, ORR) remain not yet reported**, with anticipated posting June 2027. The open-label or investigator-assessed nature of EFS in early hematologic responses, combined with immature overall survival data and the 67% higher mortality rate in the placebo arm at data cutoff, raises questions about the durability of the EFS benefit and baseline prognostic balance. The increased rates of QT prolongation and differentiation syndrome in the AG-120 arm are noteworthy. This trial is registrational for an IDH1 inhibitor in combination with azacitidine for newly diagnosed non-intensive AML, but the clinical utility cannot be fully assessed until OS, CR, and ORR data are published.

**Caveats:**  
- **Surrogate-only endpoint (so far):** EFS is a composite endpoint combining treatment failure (early endpoint), relapse, and death. OS and CR rate—the gold-standard efficacy measures in AML—are not yet reported.  
- **Early data; immature follow-up:** With OS anticipated June 2027, the median follow-up is short relative to typical OS assessment windows in AML.  
- **High mortality differential:** Placebo arm mortality (62%) vs. AG-120 arm (38%) at this cutoff is substantial; unclear if this reflects true efficacy or selection/baseline imbalance.  
- **QT prolongation signal:** 20% of AG-120 patients vs. 7% placebo experienced QT prolongation; clinical significance (e.g., Torsades de Pointes) not detailed here.  
- **Differentiation syndrome:** More frequent in AG-120 arm (8% vs. 1%); management and severity not detailed in this summary.

## Trial: Tipifarnib in Treating Patients With Acute Myeloid Leukemia in Remission

**NCT number:** NCT00093470  
**Title:** A Phase III Randomized Study of Farnesyl Transferase Inhibitor R115777 in Acute Myeloid Leukemia (AML) Patients in Second or Subsequent Remission or in Remission After Primary Induction Failure or Patients Over Age 60 in First Remission  
**Journal/source:** ClinicalTrials.gov  
**Study first posted:** 2004-10-08  
**Results first posted:** 2015-11-10  
**Study status:** COMPLETED  

### Population & design
- **Phase:** III, randomized
- **Design:** Open-label randomized comparison vs. observation
- **N enrolled:** 144 (73 tipifarnib, 71 observation)
- **Disease/setting:** AML in remission (second or subsequent remission, remission after primary induction failure, or first remission in patients age ≥60)
- **Key eligibility:** Adult AML patients meeting remission criteria; multiple AML morphologies and cytogenetic subtypes included (see conditions list)

### Interventions
- **Arm A (experimental):** Tipifarnib (R115777 / Zarnestra) 300 mg PO BID on days 1–21; courses repeat every 28 days in absence of disease progression or unacceptable toxicity (N = 73)
- **Arm B (control):** Clinical observation only (N = 71)

### Primary endpoint
**Disease-free Survival (DFS)**  
Defined as time from randomization to relapse or death without relapse.

- **Tipifarnib:** median 8.87 months (95% CI 5.85–13.34)
- **Observation:** median 5.26 months (95% CI 3.88–7.56)
- **Hazard ratio:** 0.76 (95% CI 0.538–1.072)
- **p-value:** 0.026 (one-sided); study threshold for statistical significance was p < 0.025
- **Statistical method:** Stratified log-rank test

### Secondary endpoint
**Overall Survival (OS)**  
Defined as time from randomization to death from any cause.

- **Tipifarnib:** median 16.36 months (95% CI 12.26–25.20)
- **Observation:** median 9.27 months (95% CI 8.41–19.88)
- **Hazard ratio:** 0.809 (95% CI 0.567–1.155)
- **p-value:** 0.056 (one-sided); study threshold p < 0.025
- **Statistical method:** Stratified log-rank test

### Safety (grade ≥3 adverse events)
**Serious adverse events:**
- Tipifarnib arm (72 at risk): 52 patients with ≥1 serious AE; observation arm (69 at risk): 8 patients
- Notable serious AEs favoring observation: white blood cell decreased (tipifarnib 29 vs. observation 2); neutrophil count decreased (25 vs. 0); platelet count decreased (36 vs. 6); anemia (9 vs. 2); febrile neutropenia (2 vs. 0); infections (varied); acute coronary syndrome (1 vs. 0); psychiatric events (2 confusion, 1 agitation, 1 psychosis vs. 0); peripheral sensory neuropathy (1 vs. 0); treatment-related secondary malignancy (1 vs. 0)

**Other (non-serious) adverse events:**
- Tipifarnib: 50 patients with ≥1 non-serious AE; observation: 23 patients
- Most common non-serious AEs in tipifarnib arm: anemia (40), white blood cell decreased (42), platelet count decreased (39), all more frequent than observation

**Median follow-up:** Not reported  
**Funding source:** National Cancer Institute (NCI) / NIH  
**Notable conflicts:** NCI-sponsored; no industry sponsor involvement stated  

### Potential practice relevance
Farnesyl transferase inhibitor tipifarnib failed its primary objective: DFS improvement was marginally above statistical threshold (p = 0.026 vs. prespecified p < 0.025) with a 95% CI crossing 1.0. OS (secondary endpoint) did not reach statistical significance (p = 0.056). Substantially higher grade ≥3 hematologic and non-hematologic toxicity in the tipifarnib arm without confirmed OS benefit. Data from 2015; modern standards (FLT3, NPM1, TP53 status, IDH inhibitors, venetoclax) were not available at randomization.

**Caveats:**
- Primary endpoint p-value marginally exceeded prespecified threshold; 95% CI for DFS HR includes 1.0
- OS (the clinically definitive endpoint) did not achieve statistical significance and is underpowered relative to DFS
- Study completed over a decade ago; landscape of AML therapy has evolved substantially
- Open-label design with DFS as primary endpoint (investigator-assessed, potentially prone to bias in favor of active treatment)
- Recruitment targeted a heterogeneous remission population; modern prognostication by molecular features would likely stratify differently
