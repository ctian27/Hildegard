**Title:** A randomized trial of GVHD prophylaxis in haploidentical PBSC transplantation: ATG, PTCy, and low-dose combination therapy.
**Journal/source:** Blood | **Date:** 2026-Jun-25 | **PMID:** 41849227 | **DOI:** 10.1182/blood.2025032569 | **NCT:** NCT03608059

**Disease / setting / line of therapy:** Acute myeloid leukemia or myelodysplastic syndrome with excess blasts I or II, undergoing haploidentical peripheral blood stem cell transplantation; GVHD prophylaxis setting.

**Phase | design | N enrolled:** Phase III, open-label, randomized (2:1:1), multicenter. N = 407 (ATG/PTCy 185; ATG 113; PTCy 109).

**Population / key eligibility:** Patients aged 14–70 years with AML or MDS-EB1/EB2 undergoing haploidentical PBSC transplantation.

**Intervention vs comparator:** Low-dose ATG (5 mg/kg) + PTCy (50 mg/kg) ["ATG/PTCy"] vs standard-dose ATG (total 10 mg/kg) vs PTCy-based regimen (total 100 mg/kg), all for GVHD prophylaxis.

**Primary endpoint + result:**
- Cumulative incidence (CI) of grade 2–4 acute GVHD by day 100: not significantly different among the 3 groups (P = .210). Per-group point estimates: not reported.
- GVHD-free, relapse-free survival (GRFS) at 1 year: not reported (no numeric values or CI/p-value given in abstract).

**Key secondary endpoints:**
- Overall chronic GVHD incidence: comparable across groups (P = .110); point estimates not reported.
- 2-year CI of moderate-to-severe chronic GVHD: ATG/PTCy 17.4%, ATG 17.3%, PTCy 28.3% (not statistically significant, P = .095).
- Neutrophil and platelet recovery CI: significantly higher in ATG/PTCy group than others (P < .001); exact values not reported.
- Survival outcomes: "No significant differences were observed in survival outcomes among the 3 groups" — explicit OS data (point estimates, HR/CI, p-value) not reported in abstract; OS not stated as reached/immature.

**Safety:** Grade ≥3 AEs of note: not reported. Treatment-related deaths: not reported. Discontinuations: not reported.

**Median follow-up:** not reported.

**Funding source / notable conflicts of interest:** not reported.

**Potential practice relevance:** Suggests low-dose ATG/PTCy, standard-dose ATG, and PTCy-based regimens yield similar rates of grade 2–4 acute GVHD and comparable survival in haploidentical PBSC transplant for AML/MDS, with faster count recovery in the ATG/PTCy arm and a numerically (non-significant) lower rate of moderate-to-severe chronic GVHD with ATG-containing regimens. Caveats: open-label design with an investigator-assessed/subjective endpoint (GVHD grading); coprimary GRFS and other survival data not quantified in the abstract; the cGVHD difference (17% vs 28%) is clinically notable but did not reach significance (P = .095), raising a possible power/sample-size limitation; unequal group sizes (2:1:1) and abstract's framing of "similar efficacy" despite this near-significant difference warrant reading the full text before drawing conclusions.
